KNOW THE SIGNS

RECOGNIZE & PREVENT EXPLOITATION

Understanding how online exploitation happens is the first line of defense for protecting children.

01

Online Grooming

Grooming is when an adult builds trust with a child online with intent to exploit them sexually. It happens gradually through games, social media, or messaging — disguised as friendship.

02

Protecting Children Online

Enable parental controls on all devices. Have open conversations about online safety. Know what platforms your children use and set rules about sharing personal information.

03

Sextortion

Sextortion occurs when someone threatens to share intimate images of a minor unless demands are met. This growing crime affects children aged 10–17. Immediate reporting is critical.

04

Understanding CSAM

Child Sexual Abuse Material is any content that sexually depicts a minor. Viewing, sharing, storing or producing it is a serious criminal offense globally — including illustrated or AI-generated content.

05

Reporting Is Mandatory

In many jurisdictions discovering child abuse material creates a legal reporting obligation. Do NOT download or preserve material — note the URL and platform, then report immediately.

06

Talk to Children

Trusted adults never ask children for secrets, photos, or personal information online. If anything makes them uncomfortable online, they can tell a trusted adult without fear. Safety first.

⚠ WARNING SIGNS A CHILD MAY BE AT RISK
Becoming secretive about online activities or hiding their screen
Unexplained gifts, money, or new devices from unknown sources
Switching apps or closing browser quickly when adults approach
Withdrawal from family, friends, or regular activities
Emotional distress or mood changes after online use
Using devices secretly late at night or in hidden locations
Mentioning new older friends met online they're reluctant to discuss
Unexplained knowledge of sexual topics beyond their age

This platform never stores, hosts, or displays CSAM. All uploads are scanned using PhotoDNA hash-matching. Illegal content is blocked and the attempt automatically reported to law enforcement.

All reports are transmitted using TLS 1.3 and stored with AES-256 encryption. Only authorized law enforcement partners can access submitted reports. Anonymous submissions use zero-knowledge architecture.

When anonymous mode is enabled, no identifying information is collected. IP addresses are never logged for anonymous submissions. Your identity is never shared except as required by law.

04

Direct Law Enforcement Routing

Every report is forwarded to NCMEC, IWF, national cybercrime units, and international law enforcement. Reports are triaged by urgency and jurisdiction for the fastest possible response.

05

Data Retention Policy

Report data retained for 7 years per law enforcement agreements, then permanently deleted. Anonymous reports contain zero personal identifiers. Full GDPR and COPPA compliance maintained.

Good Faith Protection

Reporting in good faith is legally protected in most jurisdictions. You cannot be held liable for reporting content you genuinely believed to be illegal. False reports may constitute a criminal offense.
